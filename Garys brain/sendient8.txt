Cold Call Script – Law Firms
Goal: Get past the gatekeeper or secure a 15-minute follow-up. Not to sell. Not to explain your life story.
1. Gatekeeper Version (Receptionist / Assistant)

“Hi, this is [Your Name]. I’m calling with a quick question about how the firm handles delivery of privileged documents. Who’s the right person for me to speak with about that?”

Stop. Do not explain. Silence is your ally.
If they ask why:

“We work with firms on verifiable chain-of-custody for sensitive deliveries. I don’t need to pitch them, I just need to confirm who owns that process.”

You are not asking for a favor. You are categorizing responsibility.

2. Decision-Maker Version (Managing Partner / Ops Director)

“Hi [Name], this is [Your Name]. I’ll be brief.
I work with law firms on delivery of privileged documents where the issue isn’t speed, it’s proof. I’m calling because most firms I speak with are spending serious time and money trying to document chain-of-custody after the fact, and I wanted to see if that’s something you deal with internally.”

Then stop talking.
If they say yes / sort of / maybe:

“That tracks. The reason firms talk to us is usually malpractice risk, not logistics. We’re not a courier. We’re a documentation layer that creates an immutable audit trail for delivery so firms don’t have to defend it later.”


3. The Objection Pre-Emption (Before They Say “We Use FedEx”)
If they say “We already have couriers”:

“Most of our clients did too. This usually isn’t about replacing them. It’s about what happens when opposing counsel challenges delivery or receipt and the tracking record isn’t defensible.”

This reframes without attacking their competence.

4. The Ask (Very Specific, Very Small)

“I’m not asking you to decide anything on this call.
Would it make sense to spend 15 minutes seeing how firms are handling this now and whether it’s even relevant for you?”

If they hesitate:

“Totally fine if it’s not. I just don’t want to be the person who assumed it wasn’t an issue when it actually is.”

That line works because it shifts risk of dismissal back onto them.

5. If You Get a Hard No

“Understood. Before I let you go, is there anyone else at the firm who handles delivery documentation or risk mitigation that I should speak with instead?”

No emotion. No scrambling. Clean exit.

That chain-of-custody gap creates risk. In law, it can trigger privilege disputes, malpractice exposure, and reputational harm. In real estate, a disputed document can cause compliance issues, failed closings, and financial loss. In jewelry and high-value goods, weak custody records plus insurance friction amplify theft risk, claim disputes, and cash-flow shocks. Teams patch this with spreadsheets, emails, screenshots and follow-up calls—costly error-prone work that still isn’t audit-ready when challenged.
